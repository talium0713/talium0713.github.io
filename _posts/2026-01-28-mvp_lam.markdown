Multi-View Point Latent Action Model (MVP-LAM) learns discrete latent actions that are highly informative about ground-truth actions from time-synchronized multi-view videos. By training with a cross-viewpoint reconstruction objective, where a latent action inferred from one view must explain the future in another view, MVP-LAM reduces reliance on viewpoint-specific cues and produces more action-centric representations. On Bridge V2 it achieves higher mutual information with ground-truth actions and improved action prediction, and pretraining VLAs with MVP-LAM latent actions improves downstream manipulation performance on the SIMPLER and LIBERO-Long benchmarks.
